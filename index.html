Transmissions // 2100

Nobody knows what the 22nd century actually holds. Here's my honest, informed guess at a few threads worth building toward now — logged like intercepts from a future that hasn't happened yet.

01 · INTERFACES

Intent replaces syntax

Engineers increasingly describe outcomes and constraints; systems handle implementation. Writing code by hand becomes a specialist skill, like assembly is today — not extinct, just niche.

02 · ENERGY

Cheap, dense power reshapes what's possible

Advances in fusion and next-gen solar/storage make compute and manufacturing dramatically less energy-constrained — changing which ideas are economically viable to build.

03 · BIOLOGY

Software eats biology, properly this time

AI-assisted protein and drug design, plus cheaper genomic tooling, turn medicine into something closer to an engineering discipline — with all the new failure modes that implies.

04 · INFRASTRUCTURE

Compute becomes a utility, not a product

Distributed, verifiable compute networks — likely including off-world data infrastructure — make raw processing power as unremarkable as electricity is now.

05 · WORK

The job is judgment, not output

As generation gets cheap, the scarce skill becomes knowing what's worth building, what's true, and what's safe to ship — taste and responsibility, not typing speed.

06 · CITIES

Cities get quietly re-engineered

Carbon-aware materials, automated logistics, and climate adaptation reshape urban design — less "flying cars," more infrastructure that finally stops fighting the planet.

" The best way to predict the future is to build it. "
